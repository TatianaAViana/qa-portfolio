# Risk Management Basics (Software Testing)

## 1. Basic Concepts

A risk refers to the possibility of something good or bad happening.  
In testing, we focus only on the possibility of negative outcomes.  

The goal is to identify as many risks as possible related to the software and then mitigate them.

Mitigation is the attempt to prevent a risk from becoming an actual problem.


## 2. Identifying Risks

Risk identification can be done individually or in groups.

Example (after discussion between QA, PO, and Tech Lead):

R1: Unauthorized users gaining access to valid user data  
R2: Clients using Safari and Firefox cannot use the application  
R3: Website login is not accessible, blocking 24% of users  


## 3. Classifying Risks

After identifying risks, we classify them using:

- Impact (I): level of damage caused  
- Probability (P): likelihood of occurrence  

Scale: 1 (very low) to 5 (very high)

R1: P=4, I=5 → Unauthorized data access  
R2: P=5, I=1 → Browser incompatibility  
R3: P=2, I=3 → Login not accessible  


## 4. Risk Level

Risk Level = Impact × Probability

R1: 4 × 5 = 20  
R2: 5 × 1 = 5  
R3: 2 × 3 = 6  


## 5. Prioritizing Risks

Risks should be prioritized based on their level:

1. R1 (20) → Highest priority  
2. R3 (6)  
3. R2 (5) → Lowest priority  


## Summary

- Identify risks  
- Classify using Impact and Probability  
- Calculate Risk Level  
- Prioritize based on severity
